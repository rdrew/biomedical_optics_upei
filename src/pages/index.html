--- title: Biomedical Optics ---

The Biomedical Optics Project combines groundbreaking research in two emerging areas of medical technology – optoacoustic imaging and laser thermal therapy – to develop new prostate cancer treatment technologies which can be precisely targeted to destroy tumours without harming adjacent tissues. The emerging multi-disciplinary field of biomedical optics uses light and other forms of radiant energy to image, analyze and manipulate living tissue at cellular and molecular levels, in a non-invasive or minimally invasive manner. The enormous potential of this technology to promote human and animal health is being pursued by a rapidly growing industry, focused on translating scientific discoveries around lasers, light and imaging into useful medical tools.

The project links the University of Prince Edward Island (UPEI) Department of Physics and the Atlantic Veterinary College (AVC) with Seno Medical Instruments of San Antonio Texas, Princess Margaret Hospital and Ryerson University of Toronto into a dynamic partnership. The target deliverables at project completion are prototype devices, instruments, and data demonstrating proof of principle of the application of fibre-optic based tissue characterization and Seno’s globally patented optoacoustic diagnostic imaging technology to monitoring of thermal cancer therapy. The research may result in the integration of these two leading-edge technologies into a groundbreaking new cancer treatment modality, with potential for further applications to small animal research, ischemic stroke, and development of nutraceuticals and pharmaceuticals.
